## 1.1 The Problem We Solve

Every offer starts with a problem. The deeper you understand the problem, the easier the sale. Trixode solves problems at three layers. Learn all three. Use the surface layer to start conversations. Use the deeper layer to build urgency. Use the root cause to close deals.

### Surface Layer (What They Say)

These are the words prospects actually use. You will hear these on calls, in emails, and in discovery meetings.

- "Our website looks outdated."
- "We don't show up on Google."
- "We get traffic but no leads."
- "I'm spending money on ads but the landing page doesn't convert."
- "My team wastes hours on tasks that should be automated."
- "I need a new website but the last agency took 4 months and it still doesn't work."

### Deeper Layer (What They Feel)

Behind every surface complaint is an emotional reality. This is where buying decisions actually happen.

- **Embarrassment**: Their website makes their business look smaller and less credible than it is. They cringe when they send prospects to it.
- **Frustration with wasted money**: They paid an agency or freelancer before and got a pretty design that generated zero leads. They feel burned.
- **Anxiety about falling behind**: Their competitors have better sites, rank higher on Google, and seem to be winning the digital game. The gap is widening.
- **Overwhelm**: They know they need to "do something digital" but they don't know what to prioritize, who to trust, or how to evaluate options.
- **Lost control**: Their lead flow depends on referrals or a single channel. They have no system. If referrals dry up, revenue drops and they have no lever to pull.

### Root Cause (The Real Problem)

This is what you solve. Not websites. Not SEO. Not AI agents. You solve this:

**Most SMBs have no integrated growth system.** They have a website from one vendor, maybe some SEO from another, manual follow-up processes that leak leads, and zero visibility into what is actually working. The result is unpredictable revenue, wasted marketing spend, and a growing gap between them and competitors who figured this out.

Trixode replaces the patchwork with one integrated system: a conversion-first website that captures leads, SEO that drives qualified traffic, and AI automation that responds faster than any human team. One strategy, one build, one dashboard, one accountable partner.

**Use this language on calls:**

> "Most businesses we talk to don't actually have a website problem or an SEO problem. They have a system problem. Their website was built by one person, their SEO is handled by another, their follow-up is manual, and nobody is looking at the whole picture. That's what we fix. We build the entire growth engine as one system so every piece works together."

**WHY THIS WORKS:** This reframe elevates you above every other web design shop competing on features and price. You are not selling a website. You are selling a growth system. That is a different category with different economics. A $5,000 website feels expensive. A $5,000 growth system that generates $10,000+/month in pipeline feels like a bargain.

## 1.5 Price Anchoring Strategy

Price anchoring works because the human brain doesn't evaluate prices in a vacuum. It evaluates prices relative to the last number it heard. Control the anchors and you control how the price feels.

### Five Anchoring Tactics with Scripts

| Tactic | When to Use | Script |
|---|---|---|
| **Status-quo cost anchor** | When prospect admits to losing leads | "You mentioned losing roughly [X] leads per month. If each lead is worth [LEAD_VALUE] CAD in lifetime value, that's [X * LEAD_VALUE] CAD per year walking out the door. The Lead Engine is a one-time 4,999 investment to fix that permanently." |
| **Competitor spend anchor** | When prospect is comparing options | "Most businesses end up paying 8,000-15,000 for just a website from a traditional agency, then another 1,500/month for SEO from a separate vendor, then 3,000+ for a chatbot. That's 25,000+ in the first year alone. We deliver all three as one integrated system starting at 4,999." |
| **Time anchor** | When prospect is considering DIY or internal | "You could spend the next 6 months trying to piece this together internally. Or we ship a working system in 2-3 weeks. The question is what 5 months of lost leads costs your business." |
| **Risk anchor** | When prospect is nervous about investment | "With our 30-day satisfaction guarantee, you're not betting 4,999 on an unknown outcome. You're investing 4,999 with a guaranteed safety net. If it doesn't work, you're protected." |
| **Stacked value anchor** | When presenting the offer | "Everything in the Lead Engine -- the website, the AI chatbot, the SEO foundation, the CRM integration, the 60-day support -- would cost roughly 14,000 if you sourced each piece separately. Your investment is 4,999. That's 64% below what you'd pay anywhere else, and you get it as one integrated system." |

### Verbal Value Stack Delivery Script

Use this exact structure when presenting any package on a call:

> "[FIRST_NAME], let me walk you through exactly what's included and what it's worth."
>
> "First, the core build -- [DESCRIBE CORE]. If you hired a specialist agency for just this piece, you'd pay [CORE_VALUE]."
>
> "On top of that, we include [BONUS_1] -- that's worth [VALUE_1] separately."
>
> "We also add [BONUS_2] -- another [VALUE_2] in standalone value."
>
> "And [BONUS_3] -- [VALUE_3]."
>
> "Total implementation value across all of that: roughly [TOTAL_VALUE] CAD."
>
> [pause]
>
> "Your investment for the complete system is [PRICE]. And it comes with [GUARANTEE_NAME] so risk stays on our side, not yours."
>
> [pause -- let them process]
>
> "Does that feel proportional to the [OUTCOME] you described earlier?"

**WHY THIS WORKS:** You are stacking value one component at a time, anchoring each against its standalone market price. By the time you say the actual price, the prospect has heard a much larger number multiple times. The price feels small by comparison. The final question ties back to their stated outcome, not the price -- this keeps the conversation about value, not cost.

**Rule: Always deliver the guarantee AFTER the value stack, BEFORE the price.** This sequence creates maximum perceived safety.

## 1.9 Payment Plan Structures

**Rule: NEVER discount the core price. Adjust terms, not value.** Payment plans cost slightly more than pay-in-full to reward decisive buyers and cover payment risk.

### How to Present Payment Plans

Always present pay-in-full first. Then offer alternatives only if the prospect hesitates on price.

> "Investment for the Lead Engine is 4,999 -- that's the best rate when you pay in full at kickoff. If cash flow is a factor, we also have a two-payment option: 2,999 to start, then 2,250 before launch. Or if you prefer the lowest entry point, we can split it three ways. **WHY THIS WORKS:** Offering three options shifts the question from "should I buy?" to "which option should I pick?" The pay-in-full anchor makes the split plans feel like accommodations, not discounts. And you never reduce the core value -- you only adjust the payment timing.

## 2.3 Inbound Discovery Call Script (15 Minutes)

The discovery call is where deals are won or lost. This is not a pitch. It is a diagnostic conversation where you help the prospect understand the cost of their current situation, then prescribe the right solution using their own words.

### Pre-Call Checklist (Complete 5 Minutes Before Every Call)

- [ ] Pull up their website on your screen (have it ready to share)
- [ ] Run their site through Google PageSpeed Insights -- note mobile score
- [ ] Review your research notes from the 5-minute audit
- [ ] Check their Google reviews (count, rating, any patterns)
- [ ] Open a blank doc for call notes
- [ ] Have the pricing/value stack table ready but NOT visible to the prospect yet
- [ ] Have your Calendly link ready for next-step booking
- [ ] Know which offer tier is the likely recommendation based on your research

### The 15-Minute Flow

| Segment | Time | Objective |
|---|---:|---|
| Opening | 0:00-2:00 | Set agenda, get permission to lead |
| Diagnosis | 2:00-7:00 | Understand pain, economics, urgency |
| Pivot | 7:00-10:00 | Match offer to problem with live ROI math |
| Close | 10:00-13:00 | Investment, process, next step |
| Buffer | 13:00-15:00 | Questions, scheduling, wrap-up |

### Opening Script (0:00-2:00)

> "[FIRST_NAME], thanks for making time. Here's what I suggest: I'll ask a few quick questions to understand your current lead flow and where the bottlenecks are. Then, if there's a fit, I'll walk you through the fastest path to fix it. And then we'll decide together if it makes sense to move forward now, later, or not at all. Sound good?"

**WHY THIS WORKS:** Three things happen in this opener. First, you set the agenda so you control the conversation -- you are the doctor, not the salesperson. Second, "if there's a fit" signals you might NOT sell them anything, which lowers their guard. Third, "now, later, or not at all" gives them explicit permission to say no, which paradoxically makes them more likely to say yes.

### Diagnosis Questions (2:00-7:00)

Ask these in order. Each question builds on the previous answer. The annotations explain why each question matters so you understand the strategic purpose, not just the words.

| # | Question | Why You Ask This |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | "How are new leads currently coming in today -- referrals, your website, ads, or something else?" | Maps channel dependency. If they say "mostly referrals," that's a massive vulnerability you'll use later. |
| 2 | "Roughly how many leads do you get per month, and how many of those become paying clients?" | Establishes their conversion baseline. You need these numbers for the ROI math in the pivot. |
| 3 | "What's the average value of a new client to your business? First transaction, and then over a year?" | Enables you to calculate ROI in their terms. A dentist who says "$3,000/year per patient" just gave you your entire closing argument. |
| 4 | "Where do you think leads are leaking right now -- is it traffic, conversion on the site, or follow-up speed?" | Identifies the primary bottleneck. Their answer tells you which offer to recommend. |
| 5 | "What have you already tried in the last 6-12 months to fix this, and what happened?" | Avoids repeating failed approaches and reveals past disappointments you need to address. If they got burned by an agency, you must handle the trust objection proactively. |
| 6 | "If we could fix one thing in the next 30-60 days, what outcome matters most to you?" | Defines their dream outcome in their own words. You will mirror this language back in the pivot. |
| 7 | "Who besides you needs to approve this kind of decision?" | Clarifies authority. If there's a partner, you need to plan for a second call or a decision brief. Never be surprised by "let me check with my partner" at the close. |
| 8 | "What's your current lead response time -- when someone fills out a form or calls, how fast does someone get back to them?" | This is a conversion predictor. Most SMBs take 4-24 hours to respond. Research shows 5-minute response times convert 8x better. This question often creates an "aha" moment. |

### The Pivot Script (7:00-10:00)

This is where you transition from diagnosis to prescription. The key: use THEIR words, not yours.

> "Based on what you shared -- [SUMMARIZE THEIR PAIN IN THEIR WORDS] -- and a goal of [THEIR STATED OUTCOME] -- the best fit is our [OFFER_NAME]."
>
> "Here's what that includes: we'd focus first on [LEVER_1 -- tied to their #1 bottleneck], then [LEVER_2], then layer in [LEVER_3] so the gains stick after launch."
>
> "Let me show you the math on this."

**Live ROI Formula (walk them through this on the call):**

| Step | Formula | Example (Dentist) | Example (Real Estate) |
|---|---|---|---|
| Extra leads/month | `Monthly Traffic x (Target Conv% - Current Conv%)` | 500 visits x (5% - 2%) = 15 extra leads | 300 visits x (4% - 1.5%) = 7.5 extra leads |
| Extra customers/month | `Extra Leads x Close Rate` | 15 x 40% = 6 new patients | 7.5 x 30% = 2.25 extra consults |
| Revenue lift/month | `Extra Customers x Avg Client Value` | 6 x $2,500 = $15,000/month | 2.25 x $15,000 GCI = $33,750/month |
| Payback period | `Investment / Monthly Lift` | $4,999 / $15,000 = 0.33 months | $4,999 / $33,750 = 0.15 months |

**ROI Delivery Script:**

> "So right now you're getting about [CURRENT_LEADS] leads per month from your website. If we improve conversion from [CURRENT_RATE]% to [TARGET_RATE]% -- which is conservative based on what we typically see -- that's [EXTRA_LEADS] additional leads per month."
>
> "At your close rate of [CLOSE_RATE]% and an average client value of [AVG_VALUE] CAD, that's roughly [REVENUE_LIFT] CAD per month in new revenue."
>
> "The Lead Engine is a one-time investment of 4,999 CAD. Based on those numbers, you'd recoup the full investment in [PAYBACK_PERIOD]."
>
> [pause -- let them process the math]
>
> "Does that math track with how you see the opportunity?"

**WHY THIS WORKS:** You are not telling them the ROI. You are calculating it WITH them, using THEIR numbers. This makes the conclusion feel like their discovery, not your sales pitch. The final question invites them to validate the math, which psychologically commits them to the conclusion.

### The Close Script (10:00-13:00)

> "If we move forward, the investment for [OFFER_NAME] is [PRICE] CAD. Process is simple: we start with a kickoff call within 48 hours of deposit, then you see weekly progress demos, and we launch in [TIMELINE]. You're covered by our [GUARANTEE_NAME] so there's zero risk on your side."
>
> "If this feels aligned, I can send the proposal today and we'd start [SPECIFIC_DATE]. Does that work for you?"
>
> [pause -- wait for their response. Do not fill the silence.]

**If they say YES:**

> "Great. I'll have the proposal in your inbox within 2 hours. It'll include scope, timeline, investment, and the guarantee in writing. Once you approve and the deposit is in, we kick off within 48 hours. I'll block [SPECIFIC_DATE] for our kickoff call. Sound good?"

**If they say "I need to think about it":**

> "Totally fair. Usually when someone needs to think it over, it's one of three things: the timing feels off, the budget feels tight, or there's a confidence question about the result. Which one is closest for you?"
>
> [Let them answer. Then address that specific concern using the relevant objection script from Part 3.]
>
> "If helpful, I can send a one-page decision brief with the ROI math, scope, timeline, and guarantee so you can review it with [PARTNER/TEAM]. Would that help move things forward?"

**If they say NO:**

> "Appreciate you being upfront. Out of curiosity -- is it timing, budget, or just not a priority right now?"
>
> [Listen. Then:]
>
> "Fair enough. I'll add you to our quarterly check-in list so if things change, we can pick this back up. In the meantime, the three quick wins I mentioned are still valid -- feel free to action those internally. Wishing [COMPANY] a great [QUARTER/SEASON]."

- [ ] Update lead status in Streak CRM

## 2.4 Referral Request Scripts

Referrals have the highest close rate of any channel (30-50% vs 5-10% for cold outbound). The problem is most people never ask. Here are word-for-word scripts for three referral scenarios.

### Script 1: Asking an Existing Client

**When to use:** After a successful milestone delivery or at the end of a project.

**WHY THIS WORKS:** You are asking if anyone "comes to mind," not "can you refer me to someone." The first is a thinking exercise. The second is a favor request. Thinking exercises are easier to say yes to.

**WHY THIS WORKS:** Reciprocity. You are not asking for a one-way favor. You are proposing a mutual referral arrangement where both parties benefit. This creates a long-term pipeline source, not a one-time ask.

# PART 3 -- OBJECTION HANDLING ENCYCLOPEDIA

---

Every objection is a buying signal. A prospect who objects is engaged. A prospect who goes silent is lost. Your job is not to "overcome" objections -- it is to understand the real concern behind the words, address it, and help the prospect make a confident decision.

## 3.1 The ACRPC Framework

Every objection response in this playbook uses the same 5-step framework. Memorize the structure, then adapt the words to the specific situation.

```
A - ACKNOWLEDGE    Validate their concern. Never argue. Never dismiss.
C - CLARIFY        Ask a question to find the real issue behind the stated objection.
R - REFRAME        Shift the perspective so the objection looks different.
P - PROOF          Back up the reframe with evidence, data, or case study.
C - CLOSE          Move toward the next step with a specific, low-friction ask.
```

**The Cardinal Rules of Objection Handling:**

| Rule | Why |
|---|---|
| Never argue | Arguments create resistance. Questions create understanding. |
| Pause before responding | 2 seconds of silence shows you are thinking, not reacting. |
| Mirror their language | Use their exact words back to them. It signals deep listening. |
| Ask before you answer | The stated objection is almost never the real objection. Clarify first. |
| One reframe per objection | Don't stack 5 arguments. One strong reframe plus one proof point is enough. |
| Always end with a question or next step | Never leave the conversation hanging after your response. |

---

## 3.2 Price Objections

### Objection 1: "It's too expensive"

**Root Cause:** They are comparing the price to zero (doing nothing) or to a cheaper alternative without considering the value differential. The perceived value is lower than the price.

**Reframe Strategy:** Shift the comparison from price vs. zero to price vs. the cost of inaction.

| Step | Script |
|---|---|
| **Acknowledge** | "Makes sense, [FIRST_NAME]. You should protect budget and be sure this pays back." |
| **Clarify** | "When you say expensive -- are we comparing to another provider, to handling it internally, or is it a cash-flow timing issue?" |
| **Reframe** | "The real comparison isn't [PRICE] vs. zero. It's [PRICE] vs. the cost of missed leads over the next 12 months. You mentioned losing roughly [X] leads per month -- at [LEAD_VALUE] each, that's [ANNUAL_LOSS] walking away this year." |
| **Proof** | "For a similar [VERTICAL] business, improving conversion by [DELTA]% added roughly [MONTHLY_LIFT] CAD in monthly pipeline. That covered the entire project in [PAYBACK_MONTHS] months." |
| **Close** | "If we structure this as a [2-PAY or 3-PAY] plan, does that make the decision workable while the ROI builds?" |

### Objection 2: "We can't afford that right now"

**Root Cause:** Different from "too expensive." This is a cash-flow constraint, not a value judgment. They may want to buy but genuinely lack the immediate funds.

**Reframe Strategy:** Offer payment flexibility and quantify the cost of waiting.

| Step | Script |
|---|---|
| **Acknowledge** | "I hear you. Cash flow matters, especially when you're investing in growth." |
| **Clarify** | "Is this about the total amount, or the timing of when it hits your budget? Those are different problems with different solutions." |
| **Reframe** | "If timing is the issue, our 3-pay option lets you start with [LOWEST_ENTRY_AMOUNT] today. The system starts working for you immediately while you spread the investment." |
| **Proof** | "Most clients see the system generating leads within 30 days of launch. Several have told us the leads from month one more than covered the remaining payments." |
| **Close** | "Would the 3-pay option at [AMOUNT] to start make this workable within your current budget cycle?" |

### Objection 3: "Your competitor quoted us half that"

**Root Cause:** They're comparing on price alone because they don't see the differentiation yet. Or they're using the competitor quote as leverage to negotiate.

**Reframe Strategy:** Shift from price comparison to outcome comparison.

| Step | Script |
|---|---|
| **Acknowledge** | "Good -- you should compare options before making a decision." |
| **Clarify** | "Help me understand what's included in that quote. Does it include AI chatbot setup, CRM integration, SEO architecture, and post-launch support? Or is it primarily design and development?" |
| **Reframe** | "Most quotes at that price point cover the website build itself. What they don't include is the growth system around it -- the AI lead capture, the SEO foundation, the CRM integration, and the optimization after launch. Those are separate line items that typically add another [AMOUNT] on top. We bundle everything into one price and one timeline because a website without a growth system is just a digital brochure." |
| **Proof** | "We hear this a lot. Businesses who go with the cheaper build often come back 6 months later spending more to fix conversion issues, add the features they skipped, and recover from lost time. The total ends up higher." |
| **Close** | "Want me to do a side-by-side comparison showing exactly what each quote covers? That way you're comparing apples to apples." |

### Objection 4: "Can you give us a discount?"

**Root Cause:** Testing whether the price is firm. Some buyers negotiate by default. Others are genuinely price-sensitive.

**Reframe Strategy:** Never discount. Adjust terms or scope instead.

| Step | Script |
|---|---|
| **Acknowledge** | "I appreciate you asking. Every dollar matters." |
| **Clarify** | "Help me understand -- is this about the total investment, or is there a specific budget ceiling you're working within?" |
| **Reframe** | "We don't discount because the value stack is already engineered to be worth 2-3x the investment. What I can do is adjust the payment terms. Or, if we need to hit a specific number, I can adjust the scope -- but I'd want to make sure we don't cut anything that impacts the result you need." |
| **Proof** | "Every client who's invested at the full scope has seen the value. The ones who cut scope to save money often end up spending more later to add back what they removed." |
| **Close** | "Would adjusting to a 2-pay or 3-pay plan solve the issue while keeping the full scope? Or is there a specific budget number I should work toward?" |

### Objection 5: "We need to see ROI first before investing"

**Root Cause:** Low perceived likelihood of achievement. They don't believe it will work for them, so they want proof before committing.

**Reframe Strategy:** Use guarantees and phased approaches to reduce perceived risk.

| Step | Script |
|---|---|
| **Acknowledge** | "That's a smart approach. You should have confidence before you invest." |
| **Clarify** | "When you say 'see ROI first,' do you mean you want to see results before paying, or that you want confidence in the math before committing?" |
| **Reframe** | "I can walk you through the ROI math right now using your numbers. If the math doesn't work, I'll tell you -- there's no point building something that won't pay back. And our [GUARANTEE_NAME] means if we don't deliver [GUARANTEE_CONDITION], you're protected." |
| **Proof** | "Let's run it live: you said [MONTHLY_TRAFFIC] visits, [CURRENT_CONV]% conversion, and [AVG_CLIENT_VALUE] per client. If we improve conversion to [TARGET_CONV]%, that's [EXTRA_LEADS] additional leads per month. At your close rate, that's [EXTRA_REVENUE] in new revenue. Payback period: [PAYBACK_MONTHS] months." |
| **Close** | "Does that math give you the confidence to move forward? And remember -- you're backed by our [GUARANTEE_NAME] either way." |

## 3.3 Timing Objections

### Objection 8: "Let's revisit next quarter"

**Root Cause:** Procrastination disguised as planning. They agree it's valuable but aren't feeling enough urgency to act now.

**Reframe Strategy:** Quantify the cost of waiting one quarter.

| Step | Script |
|---|---|
| **Acknowledge** | "Happy to reconnect next quarter if that's the right move." |
| **Clarify** | "Before I set a reminder -- what specifically changes next quarter that makes this easier? Is it budget, bandwidth, or something else?" |
| **Reframe** | "Here's what I'd want you to consider: if your site is currently converting at [CURRENT_CONV]% and you're getting [TRAFFIC] visitors per month, that's roughly [MISSED_LEADS_PER_QUARTER] leads you'll miss over the next 90 days while waiting. At [AVG_VALUE] per client, that's [COST_OF_DELAY] in pipeline you won't capture." |

| **Close** | "What if we start the foundation now -- 48 hours to kick off, 2-3 weeks to launch -- and you're already generating ROI by next quarter instead of starting from scratch?" |

### Objection 9: "We just signed with someone else"

**Root Cause:** Genuinely committed to another provider. You can't unsell this decision in the moment.

**Reframe Strategy:** Don't compete. Plant a seed for when the current engagement ends or underperforms.

| Step | Script |

### Objection 11: "We got burned by a developer/agency before"

## 5.4 Pipeline Stages with Exit Criteria

Use these stages in Streak CRM (or any pipeline tool). Every deal must be in one stage at all times.

| Stage | Definition | Exit Criteria |
|---|---|---|
| **New Lead** | Researched and scored, not yet contacted | Enrichment complete, ICP score assigned, tier routed |
| **Contacted** | First outreach touch sent (email, DM, or both) | At least one touch delivered successfully |
| **Replied** | Any human reply received (positive, negative, or neutral) | Reply logged and categorized |
| **Qualified** | ICP fit + need + authority confirmed via conversation | Confirmed: budget range, decision maker, stated pain, timeline |
| **Call Booked** | Discovery call scheduled with confirmed calendar event | Calendar event confirmed + reminder sent |
| **Call Held** | Discovery call completed | Call notes logged with: pain summary, offer recommended, next step |
| **Proposal Sent** | Proposal delivered with decision date | Proposal sent + decision date agreed + follow-up scheduled |
| **Won** | Contract signed and initial payment received | Payment confirmed + kickoff call scheduled |
| **Lost** | Deal closed-lost with reason tagged | Reason tagged: PRICE, TIMING, NO_NEED, NO_AUTHORITY, NO_RESPONSE, COMPETITOR |
| **Nurture** | Qualified but not ready now | Moved to long-term nurture cadence (Section 2.8) |

### Stage Velocity Benchmarks

| Transition | Target Duration |
|---|---|
| Contacted → Replied | 3-7 days |
| Replied → Qualified | 1-3 days |
| Qualified → Call Booked | 1-2 days |
| Call Booked → Call Held | 2-5 days |
| Call Held → Proposal Sent | Same day to 2 days |
| Proposal Sent → Won/Lost | 3-14 days |

**Rule: Any deal stuck in a stage for 2x the target duration needs intervention.** Either re-engage with a different angle, escalate (video, call), or move to nurture.

## 5.6 Red Flag Indicators and Corrective Actions

| Red Flag | Threshold | Corrective Action |
|---|---|---|
| Email reply rate falls | `< 4%` for 2 consecutive weeks | Rebuild hooks, tighten ICP targeting, verify email list quality |
| Bounce rate rises | `> 2%` | Re-verify data through Hunter, pause weak domains, clean list |
| Positive reply rate drops | `< 25%` of replies | Improve offer relevance, add more specific proof points, test new angles |
| Call show rate drops | `< 70%` | Improve booking confirmation flow, add SMS reminders, send pre-call value |
| Proposal-to-close drops | `< 20%` | Improve qualification (don't send proposals to unqualified leads), strengthen objection handling |
| Pipeline coverage weak | `< 3x` monthly target | Increase outreach volume or improve targeting quality |
| No closes in 30 days | 0 | Mandatory win/loss review: analyze every lost deal from last 60 days, identify pattern, adjust offer or process |
| MRR attach rate drops | `< 40%` | Review upsell timing, improve retainer value proposition, introduce retainer earlier in conversation |
