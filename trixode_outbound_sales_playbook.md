## SECTION 1 — IDEAL CUSTOMER PROFILE & POSITIONING

### 1.1 Primary ICP: Local/Regional Service Businesses

| Attribute | Criteria | Why It Matters |
|---|---|---|
| **Revenue** | $500K–$5M/yr | Big enough to pay $2K–$8K, small enough to lack in-house dev |
| **Employees** | 5–50 | Decision-maker is the owner; no procurement layer |
| **Industries** | Dental, legal, HVAC, med-spa, real estate, home services, accounting | High LTV per customer → marketing ROI is obvious |
| **Geography** | GTA first, then Ontario, then Canada-wide | Local trust advantage; same timezone for calls |
| **Current Website** | Outdated (3+ yrs old), no mobile optimization, or no site at all | Clear pain = easy conversation starter |
| **Tech Sophistication** | Low–Medium | They *want* help, not a DIY tool |
| **Buyer** | Owner or Office Manager | Signs cheques, feels the pain directly |

### 1.2 Secondary ICP: B2B SaaS Startups

| Attribute | Criteria | Why It Matters |
|---|---|---|
| **Stage** | Pre-seed → Series A | Need external dev help, haven't built full teams |
| **Revenue** | $0–$2M ARR | Budget exists but is tight — value-conscious |
| **Team** | 1–15 people | No dedicated marketing/web team |
| **Industry** | Any vertical SaaS | More likely to understand and value AI automation |
| **Pain** | Need MVP, landing page, or AI feature integration | Aligns perfectly with your dev + AI stack |
| **Buyer** | Founder or CTO | Technical enough to appreciate quality |

### 1.3 Psychographic Profile

**Service Business Owner ("Dave the Dentist")**

- Works 50–60 hrs/week, wears every hat
- Knows their website sucks but "doesn't have time to deal with it"
- Got burned by a cheap freelancer or agency before (paid $3K, got garbage)
- Trusts referrals over cold pitches — *you must earn trust fast*
- Thinks in ROI: "If I spend $5K, how many new patients do I get?"
- Scrolls Facebook at 9pm, checks LinkedIn sporadically, reads email on phone

**SaaS Founder ("Sara the Startup CEO")**

- Lives on Slack, Twitter/X, and LinkedIn
- Evaluates vendors by portfolio and speed, not pitch decks
- Budget-constrained but will pay for quality that ships fast
- Wants a *partner*, not a vendor — someone who understands product
- Decision speed: 48hrs if you're good, ghosted if you're mid

### 1.4 Trigger Events — When to Strike

These are the signals that a prospect is *ready to buy now*. Prioritize leads showing these:

| Trigger Event | Where to Find It | What to Say |
|---|---|---|
| New Google reviews mentioning "hard to book online" | Google Maps alerts | *"Noticed patients are saying booking is tough — we fix that in 2 weeks."* |
| Job posting for "marketing coordinator" | Indeed, LinkedIn Jobs | *"Before you hire at $55K/yr, let me show you what $4K once can do."* |
| Competitor just launched a new site | Google Alerts, SimilarWeb | *"Your competitor [NAME] just redesigned — here's what they're doing better."* |
| Just raised funding (SaaS) | Crunchbase, Twitter | *"Congrats on the raise! Most Series A cos need a site refresh — want a quote?"* |
| Moved to a new office / opened second location | Google Maps, local news | *"New location = new patients to attract. Let's make sure they find you."* |
| Negative Better Business Bureau / Yelp reviews about service | Yelp, BBB | *"I can build an automated review funnel that buries the bad ones."* |
| Website SSL expired / broken mobile | Chrome DevTools, Lighthouse | *"Your site is showing 'Not Secure' — that kills trust. Quick fix."* |
| Running Google Ads but site has no clear CTA | Google Ads Transparency Center | *"You're paying for clicks that hit a dead end. Let's fix the landing page."* |

### 1.5 Disqualifiers — Walk Away From These

| Red Flag | Why |
|---|---|
| Revenue under $200K/yr | Can't afford even starter package |
| "My nephew can build a website" | Doesn't value professional work |
| Wants to pay < $1,000 for a full site | Price-buyer, will scope-creep you to death |
| Committee of 3+ decision-makers | Sales cycle > 60 days, not worth it at your stage |
| "We need to think about it" after 3 follow-ups | Dead lead, move on |
| Asks for free spec work upfront | Doesn't respect your time |
| Industry: cannabis, adult, gambling | Payment processing issues, brand risk |

## SECTION 2 — LEAD SOURCING SYSTEM

### 2.1 Channel Matrix

| Channel | Weekly Quota | Cost | Expected Reply Rate | Best For |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Cold Email | 50 new prospects | $0 (manual) / ~$50/mo (Instantly) | 3–8% | Service businesses at scale |
| LinkedIn DMs | 20 connection requests + 10 DMs | $0 | 10–25% | SaaS founders, warm intros |
| Google Maps Scraping | 30 businesses researched | $0 | N/A (feeds email + DM lists) | Finding local service businesses |
| Referral Asks | 3 asks/week | $0 | 30–50% | Warmest leads, highest close rate |
| Local Networking / BNI | 1 event/week | $0–$30/event | 15–20% of conversations | Trust-building, dental/legal/HVAC |
| Content Inbound (LinkedIn posts) | 3 posts/week | $0 | Inbound only | Brand building, long-term pipeline |

**Weekly Lead Target: 50 net-new leads minimum.**

> **Math:** 50 leads/week × 5% reply rate = 2.5 conversations/week × 30% close rate = ~3 deals/month. At avg. deal size $4,000 = **$12,000/month revenue.** That's the model. Consistency is the variable.

### 2.2 Research Checklist — Before You Reach Out

Complete this for *every* prospect before sending a cold email or DM:

- [ ] **Company name & website URL** — screenshot the homepage
- [ ] **Owner/decision-maker name** — LinkedIn profile found?
- [ ] **Business age** — how long have they been operating?
- [ ] **Current website audit** (30 seconds): Mobile-friendly? SSL? Load speed? Clear CTA?
- [ ] **Google reviews** — how many? Average rating? Any complaints about "can't book online"?
- [ ] **Are they running ads?** — check Google Ads Transparency Center
- [ ] **Recent trigger event?** — new location, job posting, funding, competitor move?
- [ ] **Personalization hook** — one specific thing you'll mention in your outreach
- [ ] **ICP fit score** — does this prospect match Section 1? (Yes / Maybe / No)

> **Rule:** If you can't find a personalization hook in 3 minutes, skip this lead. Spray-and-pray kills deliverability and your reputation.

## SECTION 3 — OUTBOUND SCRIPTS (HOOK / MEAT / CTA)

### 3.1 The Framework

Every outreach message — email, DM, voice note — follows this structure:

| Part | Purpose | Length | Key Rule |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Hook** | Stop the scroll, earn 3 more seconds | 1 sentence | Must be *about them*, never about you |
| **Meat** | Create curiosity + show you understand their problem | 2–3 sentences | Specific > generic. Use their name/company/data |
| **CTA** | One clear, low-friction next step | 1 sentence | Ask a question, don't make a demand |

### 3.2 Hook Templates (pick one per message)

| # | Hook Template | When to Use |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | *"Quick question about [COMPANY NAME]'s website…"* | General opener, works everywhere |
| 2 | *"Noticed [SPECIFIC ISSUE] on [COMPANY NAME].com"* | When you found a real website problem |
| 3 | *"[COMPETITOR NAME] just did something smart — thought you should see."* | Competitor trigger event |
| 4 | *"[FIRST NAME], your Google reviews say something interesting…"* | When reviews mention a problem you solve |
| 5 | *"Saw you're hiring a [ROLE] — might save you $[AMOUNT]/yr"* | Job posting trigger |
| 6 | *"This 47-second video is about [COMPANY NAME]"* | Video outreach (Loom) |
| 7 | *"Congrats on [TRIGGER EVENT] — quick idea for you"* | Funding, new location, award |

### 3.3 Meat Templates (pair with any hook)

| # | Meat Template | Best Paired With |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | *"I checked [COMPANY].com — your site loads in [X] seconds (Google recommends under 3), and on mobile the [SPECIFIC ELEMENT] is broken. That's costing you [ESTIMATED] leads/month."* | Hook 2 (specific issue) |
| 2 | *"Most [INDUSTRY] businesses in [CITY] are losing 30–40% of website visitors because their site isn't built for phones. We just helped a [SIMILAR BUSINESS] fix this and they saw [RESULT] in 6 weeks."* | Hook 1 (general) |
| 3 | *"I built a quick mockup of what [COMPANY]'s site could look like. No charge — just wanted to show you what's possible in 2 weeks."* | Hook 6 (video) |
| 4 | *"Your competitor [NAME] just launched a site with online booking and a reviews widget. Right now they're capturing the leads that can't find you online."* | Hook 3 (competitor) |
| 5 | *"Instead of hiring a full-time marketing person at $55K+/yr, our clients get a conversion-optimized site + SEO + AI chatbot for a one-time $[PRICE]. Same result, 1/10th the cost."* | Hook 5 (hiring) |

### 3.4 CTA Templates (pick one — never use two CTAs)

| # | CTA Template | Friction Level |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | *"Worth a 15-min chat this week?"* | Low |
| 2 | *"Want me to send over the mockup?"* | Very Low |
| 3 | *"Open to seeing a 2-min breakdown of what I'd change?"* | Very Low |
| 4 | *"I have 2 spots open this month — want one?"* | Medium (urgency) |
| 5 | *"If I could show you exactly how to get [X RESULT], would that be worth 15 minutes?"* | Low (value-first) |
| 6 | *"Reply 'sure' and I'll send details — no obligation."* | Lowest possible |

### 3.5 Assembled Example

> **Subject:** Quick question about Smile Dental's website
>
> Hi Dr. Patel,
>
> Noticed Smile Dental's site loads in 6.2 seconds on mobile (Google recommends under 3) and there's no online booking button above the fold. Based on your traffic, that's leaving roughly 15–20 new patient inquiries/month on the table.
>
> Worth a 15-min chat this week to see if we can fix that?
>
> — Hussien, Trixode Studios

**Word count: 62. Target: under 75.**

## SECTION 4 — COLD EMAIL SEQUENCE (5 TOUCHES)

### 4.1 Sequence Overview

| Touch | Timing | Type | Goal | Max Words |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **1** | Day 0 | Problem-aware intro | Get a reply | 75 |
| **2** | Day 3 | Value-add follow-up | Provide proof | 60 |
| **3** | Day 7 | Social proof / case study | Build credibility | 65 |
| **4** | Day 12 | Breakup + resource | Final push or nurture | 50 |
| **5** | Day 18 | Video + re-engage | Personalized Loom | 40 + video |

### 4.2 Touch 1 — Problem-Aware Intro (Day 0)

**Subject Line Variants** (A/B test — send 50% each):

- A: `[COMPANY NAME]'s website — quick question`
- B: `Noticed something on [COMPANY].com`

**Body:**
> Hi [FIRST NAME],
>
> [PERSONALIZED HOOK — reference specific issue, trigger event, or observation].
>
> [MEAT — 2 sentences explaining the problem and what it's costing them].
>
> [CTA — single low-friction question].
>
> [SIGNATURE]

**Example:**
> Hi Dr. Chen,
>
> Noticed Bright Smile Dental's homepage doesn't have an online booking button — and your Google reviews mention patients calling and getting voicemail.
>
> If even 10% of visitors leave because they can't book online, that's roughly $8K–$12K/yr in missed revenue (based on avg. patient LTV in Toronto).
>
> Worth a 15-min call to see if we can fix that in 2 weeks?
>
> — Hussien, Trixode Studios

### 4.3 Touch 2 — Value-Add Follow-Up (Day 3)

**Subject Line:** `Re: [ORIGINAL SUBJECT]` (keep the thread)

**Body:**
> Hi [FIRST NAME],
>
> Following up — didn't want this to get buried.
>
> I ran [COMPANY].com through Google's speed test. Here's what I found:
>
> - Mobile speed score: [X]/100
> - Missing: [SPECIFIC ELEMENT, e.g. online booking, SSL, mobile menu]
>
> Happy to share a quick fix list — no strings attached.
>
> [CTA]

### 4.4 Touch 3 — Social Proof (Day 7)

**Subject Line:** `How [SIMILAR BUSINESS/INDUSTRY] got [RESULT]`

**Body:**
> Hi [FIRST NAME],
>
> Quick story — we helped a [INDUSTRY] business in [CITY] go from [BEFORE STATE] to [AFTER STATE] in [TIMEFRAME].
>
> The fix wasn't complicated: [1-SENTENCE SUMMARY OF WHAT YOU DID].
>
> Thought it might be relevant for [COMPANY NAME] since [REASON].
>
> [CTA]

> **Note:** If you only have 1 client so far, use: *"We just wrapped a project for a Toronto-based business..."* — don't say "our clients" (plural) if it's not true. Authenticity > impressive-sounding lies.

### 4.5 Touch 4 — Breakup Email (Day 12)

**Subject Line:** `Closing the loop on [COMPANY NAME]`

**Body:**
> Hi [FIRST NAME],
>
> I've reached out a couple times — totally get that you're busy.
>
> I put together a free website audit checklist for [INDUSTRY] businesses: [LINK OR ATTACHMENT]
>
> If timing's ever better, I'm here. Either way, hope this helps.
>
> — Hussien

> **Why it works:** Low pressure. The resource (audit checklist) gives value even if they don't reply, and keeps you top-of-mind. Many "dead" leads resurface 2–6 months later from breakup emails.

### 4.6 Touch 5 — Video Re-Engage (Day 18)

**Subject Line:** `Made this 47-sec video for [COMPANY NAME]`

**Body:**
> Hi [FIRST NAME],
>
> Recorded a quick Loom walkthrough of what [COMPANY]'s website could look like with a few changes: [LOOM LINK]
>
> No pressure — just thought it'd be easier to show than tell.
>
> Worth a quick chat?

> **Why Touch 5 exists:** By day 18, text-only emails have exhausted their novelty. Video cuts through because (a) it proves you spent time on *them specifically*, and (b) only 2% of salespeople use video, so it's a pattern-interrupt. See Section 6 for the full Loom scripting framework.

### 4.7 Deliverability Rules

| Rule | Details |
|---|---|
| **Max sends/day** | 30–50 from primary Gmail (warm up to this over 2 weeks) |
| **Domain strategy** | When upgrading: buy `trixodestudios.co` or `.io` for cold — protect main domain |
| **SPF/DKIM/DMARC** | Set up on day 1. Non-negotiable. Google "SPF record generator" |
| **Warm-up** | Send 5/day week 1, 10/day week 2, 20/day week 3, 30/day week 4 |
| **Unsubscribe** | Include a 1-line opt-out: *"PS: Reply 'stop' if this isn't relevant"* |
| **No attachments in Touch 1** | Triggers spam filters. Use links instead |
| **Plain text > HTML** | No images, no logos, no fancy formatting. Looks like a real email |

## SECTION 6 — VIDEO OUTREACH SYSTEM

### 6.1 The "Made This For You" Framework

Video outreach is your **unfair advantage** at this stage. Less than 2% of salespeople use personalized video. Here's the system:

| Element | Details |
|---|---|
| **Tool** | Loom (free tier — 25 videos, 5 min max) |
| **Length** | 47–90 seconds (under 60 is ideal) |
| **Format** | Screen share of *their* website + your face in corner bubble |
| **When to Use** | Priority A leads (ICP score ≥ 7) AND Touch 5 emails AND LinkedIn DM 3 |
| **Thumbnail** | Custom: write their company name on a whiteboard/sticky note |

### 6.2 Loom Script Template (60 seconds)

| Timestamp | What to Say | What to Show |
|---|---|---|
| **0:00–0:10** | *"Hey [NAME], Hussien here — I made this quick video just for [COMPANY]."* | Their homepage on screen |
| **0:10–0:25** | *"I noticed [ISSUE 1] — [explain impact in 1 sentence]. And here [ISSUE 2] — [explain impact]."* | Scroll to show the issues |
| **0:25–0:40** | *"Here's what I'd change: [FIX 1] and [FIX 2]. We just did this for a similar business and [RESULT]."* | Quick mockup or competitor example |
| **0:40–0:50** | *"If this is interesting, I'd love to jump on a 15-minute call and walk through a full plan. No cost, no obligation."* | Your face (full screen, look at camera) |
| **0:50–0:60** | *"Link to my calendar is below. Talk soon, [NAME]."* | Calendly link in Loom CTA |

### 6.3 Video Outreach Rules

| Rule | Why |
|---|---|
| Say their name in the first 5 seconds | Proves it's not mass-produced |
| Show *their* screen, not yours | It's about THEM |
| Max 90 seconds | After 90s, watch-through rate drops below 30% |
| Custom thumbnail with their name | 3x higher click rate vs. auto-generated |
| Include Loom CTA button | "Book a Call" → Calendly link |
| Send between 8am–10am their timezone | Highest open rates |
| Batch record Tuesdays & Thursdays | Efficiency — do 5 videos in 30 minutes |
| Track watch time | If they watched 80%+ → Priority follow-up within 24hrs |

### 6.4 Watch-Time Follow-Up Rules

| Watch Time | Action | Timing |
|---|---|---|
| 0% (didn't click) | Re-send with different subject line | +3 days |
| 1–50% | Send DM: *"Saw you caught part of the video — any questions?"* | +24 hrs |
| 50–80% | Call or DM: *"Looked like the audit resonated — worth a quick chat?"* | +12 hrs |
| 80–100% | **Priority:** Call immediately or DM with calendar link | Same day |

## SECTION 7 — DISCOVERY CALL SCRIPT (15 MINUTES)

### 7.1 Pre-Call Prep (2 min before)

- [ ] Pull up their website on your screen
- [ ] Review your research checklist notes (Section 2.2)
- [ ] Open Google PageSpeed tab with their site loaded
- [ ] Open blank Google Doc for notes
- [ ] Have your pricing table open (Section 9) but **don't show it yet**

### 7.2 The Script

**[0:00–1:00] OPENING — Build Rapport + Set Agenda**

> *"Hey [NAME], thanks for jumping on. Before we dive in — how's your day going?"*
> *(Let them answer. Be human. 15 seconds max.)*
>
> *"Perfect. So here's what I was thinking for the next 15 minutes: I'll ask a few quick questions about [COMPANY] and your goals, share a few things I noticed on your site, and then we can figure out if it makes sense to work together. Sound good?"*

**Why you're asking:** Setting the agenda gives you control. They agreed to 15 minutes — respect it. You're also subtly getting their permission to lead the conversation.

---

**[1:00–4:00] SITUATION — Understand Their World**

> *"So [NAME], tell me a bit about [COMPANY]. How long have you been in business?"*
> *(Let them talk — 30–60 seconds.)*
>
> *"And right now, how are most of your new [CUSTOMERS/PATIENTS/CLIENTS] finding you?"*

**Why you're asking:** This reveals their current acquisition channels. If they say "word of mouth only," that's a HUGE opportunity — they have zero online infrastructure.

> *"What about your website — when was the last time it was updated?"*

**Why you're asking:** If the answer is "3+ years ago" or "I don't even know," they know it's outdated. You're letting them say it, not you.

> *"And how are you currently handling [RELEVANT PROCESS — bookings, quotes, follow-ups]?"*

**Why you're asking:** Reveals manual processes that AI automation can solve. Listen for: spreadsheets, paper forms, "my receptionist does it," or "I do it myself at 10pm."

---

**[4:00–7:00] PAIN — Make the Problem Real**

> *"So it sounds like [SUMMARIZE WHAT THEY SAID]. Here's what I'm hearing: [REFRAME AS PROBLEM]."*
>
> *"Can I share what I noticed when I looked at your site?"*
> *(Always ask permission — it builds trust and flips them into listening mode.)*
>
> *"I ran [COMPANY].com through Google's speed test. Your mobile score is [X]/100. For context, anything under 50 means roughly [Y]% of visitors are bouncing before the page even loads."*
>
> *"I also noticed [SPECIFIC ISSUE — no online booking, no SSL, broken contact form, etc.]. Based on your Google reviews, it seems like [CONNECT ISSUE TO REVIEWS IF POSSIBLE]."*

**Why you're showing data:** Numbers > opinions. You're not saying "your site is bad." You're showing them *Google* says their site is underperforming. Much harder to argue with.

> *"If I had to guess, how many potential [CUSTOMERS] do you think you might be losing each month because of this?"*

**Why you're asking:** You want THEM to quantify the pain. Whatever number they say, nod and say: *"Yeah, that tracks with what I typically see."*

---

**[7:00–10:00] IMPACT — Dollarize the Problem**

> *"So let's do some rough math. You said you might be losing [X] [CUSTOMERS/PATIENTS] per month. What's a typical [CUSTOMER] worth to you over, say, a year?"*
> *(If they don't know, help: "For most dental practices, a single patient is worth about $2,000–$3,000/year.")*
>
> *"So if we're conservative and say [X] lost customers × $[LTV] = that's roughly $[TOTAL] per year in missed revenue. And that's just from the website — not counting the manual work on [PROCESS THEY MENTIONED]."*

**Why you're doing this math:** You're building the ROI case *with their own numbers*. Now when you say your price is $4,000, the mental comparison isn't *"$4,000 vs. $0"* — it's *"$4,000 vs. $36,000 in lost revenue."*

---

**[10:00–13:00] SOLUTION — Present Without Pitching**

> *"Here's what I'd recommend — and I'll be straight with you, this might not be a fit, but let me lay it out:"*
>
> *"Phase 1 — [PRIMARY SERVICE, e.g., website redesign]. We'd [2-SENTENCE SCOPE]. This typically takes [TIMELINE]."*
>
> *"Phase 2 (optional) — [SECONDARY SERVICE, e.g., AI automation]. This is where we [1 SENTENCE ON VALUE]."*
>
> *"For businesses your size, the investment is typically between $[LOW RANGE] and $[HIGH RANGE], depending on scope. I can put together a detailed proposal if you want — usually takes me about 24 hours."*

**Why you're framing this way:** *"I'll be straight with you"* → Builds trust. *"Might not be a fit"* → Reduces pressure. Giving a range (not exact price) → Anchors without boxing you in. *"Investment"* → Never say "cost."

---

**[13:00–15:00] CLOSE — Set Next Steps**

> *"So [NAME], based on everything we talked about — does this seem like something that could help [COMPANY]?"*
> *(Pause. Let them answer. Silence is your friend.)*

**If YES:**
> *"Great. Here's what I'd suggest: I'll put together a proposal with the exact scope, timeline, and investment. I'll email it over within 24 hours. Then we jump on a quick 10-minute call on [SPECIFIC DAY] to walk through it. Does [DAY] at [TIME] work?"*

**If MAYBE / NEED TO THINK:**
> *"Totally understand. Tell you what — I'll send over the proposal anyway so you have something concrete to look at. No pressure. If it makes sense, we'll hop on another call. If not, zero hard feelings. Fair?"*

**If NO:**
> *"Appreciate you being upfront. Out of curiosity — is it timing, budget, or just not a priority right now?"*

## SECTION 8 — OBJECTION HANDLING SCRIPTS

### 8.1 Framework: Acknowledge → Clarify → Reframe → Proof → Close

Never argue. Never get defensive. Objections mean they're *interested enough to engage*.

### 8.2 Objection #1: "It's too expensive"

| Step | What to Say |
|---|---|
| **Acknowledge** | *"I totally hear you — $[PRICE] is a real investment, especially for a business your size."* |
| **Clarify** | *"When you say it's too much — is it the total amount, or more about cash flow and timing?"* |
| **Reframe** | *"You mentioned losing roughly [X] customers/month. At $[LTV] per customer, that's $[ANNUAL LOSS]/year. This project pays for itself in [X] months."* |
| **Proof** | *"Our last client invested $4,000 and saw [RESULT] within 6 weeks."* |
| **Close** | *"Would a payment plan help? We can do [PAYMENT STRUCTURE — see Section 9.5]."* |

### 8.3 Objection #2: "I need to think about it"

| Step | What to Say |
|---|---|
| **Acknowledge** | *"Of course — this isn't a small decision."* |
| **Clarify** | *"What specifically do you need to think through? Scope, timing, or something else?"* |
| **Reframe** | *"Usually there's one specific thing holding someone back. If I can address it now, it saves you the mental load."* |
| **Proof** | *"[NAME] from [COMPANY] told me the same thing — 6 weeks later, they said it was the best business decision this year."* |
| **Close** | *"I'll send the proposal — let's schedule a 10-min call for [DATE]. If it's a no, just tell me. Deal?"* |

### 8.4 Objection #3: "We already have a web guy / agency"

| Step | What to Say |
|---|---|
| **Acknowledge** | *"Good — means you already know the value of having a strong web presence."* |
| **Clarify** | *"Are you 100% happy with what they're delivering, or is there a gap?"* |
| **Reframe** | *"Most sites are built by developers, not by someone thinking about conversions. We obsess over 'does this site actually get customers.'"* |
| **Proof** | *"We audited a site another agency built — looked nice but loaded in 8 seconds and had no clear CTA. Fixed both in 2 weeks."* |
| **Close** | *"Would it hurt to get a free second opinion? 5-minute audit — if everything checks out, great."* |

### 8.5 Objection #4: "Not the right time"

| Step | What to Say |
|---|---|
| **Acknowledge** | *"Makes sense — timing is everything."* |
| **Clarify** | *"When would be the right time? What would need to be different?"* |
| **Reframe** | *"Every month you wait = ~$[MONTHLY LOSS] in missed [CUSTOMERS]. In 3 months, that's $[3X LOSS]."* |
| **Proof** | *"Most clients say 'I should've done this a year ago.'"* |
| **Close** | *"What if we started with just [SMALLEST SCOPE] — biggest wins fast, without the full commitment?"* |

### 8.6 Objection #5: "Can you guarantee results?"

| Step | What to Say |
|---|---|
| **Acknowledge** | *"Great question — I respect you for asking."* |
| **Clarify** | *"What kind of results are you hoping to see?"* |
| **Reframe** | *"I CAN guarantee: sub-3-second load times, mobile-optimized, conversion-focused CTAs. I give you the machine — you add the fuel."* |
| **Proof** | *"For our last client, we tracked 90 days post-launch: [SPECIFIC METRIC]."* |
| **Close** | *"If the site doesn't perform in 90 days, I'll spend 2 weeks optimizing it free of charge. Fair?"* |

### 8.7 Objection #6: "Let me check with my partner / spouse"

| Step | What to Say |
|---|---|
| **Acknowledge** | *"Absolutely — important decisions should be made together."* |
| **Clarify** | *"Is your partner involved day-to-day, or is this more of a financial discussion?"* |
| **Reframe** | *"Would it help if I put together a 1-page summary? Bullet points, numbers, timeline — no fluff."* |
| **Proof** | *"What partners usually want: how much, how long, and ROI. The proposal covers all three."* |
| **Close** | *"Want to schedule a call where they can join? Even 10 minutes."* |

### 8.8 Quick-Reference Objection Matrix

| Objection | Root Cause | Key Reframe | Offer |
|---|---|---|---|
| Too expensive | Price anxiety | ROI math | Payment plan |
| Need to think | Decision paralysis | Remove ambiguity | Scheduled follow-up |
| Have an agency | Switching cost | Free audit, no risk | Second opinion |
| Not right time | No urgency | Cost of delay | Starter package |
| Guarantee results | Risk aversion | Control vs. contribution | 90-day guarantee |
| Check with partner | Shared decision | 1-pager for them | Partner call |

## SECTION 10 — FOLLOW-UP SYSTEM

### 10.1 Post-Proposal Follow-Up Cadence

You sent the proposal. Now what? Most deals are won or lost in the follow-up, not the pitch.

| Day | Action | Channel | Message |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Day 0** | Send proposal + confirmation email | Email | *"Hi [NAME], proposal attached. I've blocked off [DATE/TIME] for our walkthrough call. Looking forward to it."* |
| **Day 1** | Soft check-in | LinkedIn DM | *"Hey [NAME], just sent over the proposal — let me know if you have any Qs before our call on [DATE]!"* |
| **Day 3** | Proposal walkthrough call | Zoom/Phone | Use Section 7 closing framework. Ask: *"What jumped out to you?"* |
| **Day 5** | Follow-up if no response | Email | *"Hi [NAME], wanted to make sure you got the proposal. Any questions I can clear up?"* |
| **Day 8** | Value bump | Email | Share relevant resource: *"Saw this article on [TOPIC] — thought it was relevant to what we discussed."* |
| **Day 12** | Direct ask | Email/Phone | *"Hey [NAME], just checking in — are we moving forward, or has the timing changed? Either way is totally fine."* |
| **Day 18** | Breakup | Email | *"Hi [NAME], I haven't heard back so I'll assume the timing isn't right. If anything changes, I'm here. In the meantime, here's [FREE RESOURCE]. Wishing [COMPANY] all the best."* |

> **Rule:** After the breakup email, STOP. Move them to the nurture list. Do NOT send a 7th, 8th, 9th email. You'll look desperate and damage your brand.

### 10.2 "Not Right Now" Nurture Cadence
